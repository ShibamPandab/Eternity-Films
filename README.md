# ETERNITY FILMS
### A Cinematic Wedding Films Portfolio — Crafted in Black & Gold

*"We don't capture events. We preserve emotions."*

## I. Hero Introduction

Eternity Films is a luxury digital portfolio built for a cinematic wedding filmmaking studio — a space where every scroll feels like the opening frame of a film. The site opens in near-total darkness, with a single gold monogram tracing itself onto the screen before the first frame of footage begins to breathe. From there, the experience unfolds the way a wedding film does: quiet introductions, sweeping visuals, intimate close-ups, and a closing credit sequence that lingers.

This is not a typical "scroll-and-read" website. It is a sequence of curated rooms — a Netflix-style film grid, a scroll-driven love story timeline, a floating editorial gallery, a behind-the-scenes founder story, and an ambient Memory Wall with its own soundtrack — each designed to feel like part of a continuous cinematic reel rather than a stack of webpage sections.

---

## II. Project Overview

**Eternity Films** is a single-page React application representing the digital home of a fictional husband-and-wife filmmaking duo, **Raghav & Ananya**, who specialize in destination wedding cinematography across palaces, coastlines, deserts, and mountain villages. The site exists to do one thing: make a visitor *feel* the quality of the work before they ever press play.

Every section of the site has been treated as its own editorial spread — given its own pacing, its own typographic rhythm, and its own visual texture — while remaining tied together by a single, disciplined palette of deep charcoal black, warm champagne gold, and ivory.

The result is a portfolio that reads less like a "wedding vendor website" and more like the digital companion to a printed luxury wedding chronicle.

## III. Experience Highlights

**The Opening Frame (Hero)**
A full-bleed looping film plays behind a slow cinematic zoom, framed top and bottom by black letterbox bars. The headline — *"We Preserve Emotions"* — fades in line by line, followed by two calls to action: **Watch Film** and **Book Your Date**. A breathing scroll indicator anchors the bottom of the screen.

**Featured Films — Curated Masterpieces**
A Netflix-style grid of vertical film posters, each representing a real destination story — Udaipur, Lake Como, Cappadocia, the Maldives. Hovering reveals the couple's names, shoot duration, and a glowing play button. Selecting a film opens a full cinematic lightbox: video on one side, a "Now Screening" editorial card on the other, complete with location, date, duration, and a couple's quote.

**Love Stories — Scroll Storytelling Timeline**
A vertical love-story timeline that traces a couple's journey — from the bride's quiet morning portrait, to the proposal, the engagement, the vows, and the midnight reception. A gold line draws itself down the center of the page as the visitor scrolls, connecting each chapter like the spine of a storybook.

**Signature Moments — The Atelier Collection**
A floating, parallax-driven gallery of six framed photographs, each drifting gently and independently as the page scrolls. A large featured image anchors the center, surrounded by smaller frames that tilt, hover, and shift from grayscale to full color on interaction — an editorial wall brought to life.

**Wedding Films Showcase — Studio Folio**
A refined 2×3 editorial grid presenting six signature films from palaces in Jaipur to the cliffs of the Amalfi Coast, each card carrying a duration badge, location tag, and an "Explore Story" prompt that lifts gently on hover.

**Behind the Lens — The Founders**
A two-column editorial feature introducing Raghav & Ananya, paired with an offset photographic composition. Their philosophy — *"We don't script. We don't stage. We preserve raw human spirit."* — sits alongside studio statistics: years of cinema, legacies made, and countries explored.

**Experience Process — The Journey**
A five-step horizontal journey — Discovery Call, Artistic Direction, The Cinematic Shoot, The Editing Suite, and The Grand Delivery — connected by an animated gold progress line that draws itself across the screen as the visitor arrives.

**Client Reactions — Love Letters & Legacies**
A magazine-style testimonial spread with a large couple portrait on one side and an oversized editorial quote on the other, complete with "Volume" and "Page" numbering and spread navigation — designed to feel like flipping through a printed wedding chronicle.

**Instagram Wall — Social Canvas**
A Pinterest-style masonry grid of social moments, each tile revealing likes, comments, and a gold vignette overlay on hover — a living extension of the studio's social presence.

**The Memory Wall — Immersive Space**
The emotional centerpiece of the site. Photographs labeled *The Laughter*, *The Ring*, *The Waltz*, and *The Walk* drift and rotate gently around a central message: *"Because some moments deserve forever."* A floating ambient audio toggle lets visitors play a soft cinematic soundtrack as they explore — and automatically pauses whenever a film is opened elsewhere on the site.

**Book Your Date**
A refined inquiry form bordered with delicate corner brackets, capturing the couple's names, wedding date, venue, commission type, and budget range — closing with a warm, personalized confirmation message rather than a generic "submitted" notice.

## IV. Design Philosophy

The entire experience is built around restraint. Every visual decision serves the feeling of *quiet luxury* — the kind found in a well-bound photo album rather than a flashy advertisement.

**Palette** — Near-black (`#0B0B0B`, `#070707`) as the canvas, champagne gold (`#D6B37A`) as the signature accent, and warm ivory (`#F8F5F0`) for typography — never pure white, never pure black.

**Typography** — Cormorant Garamond carries every headline in elegant, editorial serif, paired with Inter for supporting text — wide letter-spacing, uppercase labels, and italic accents lend the site the rhythm of a printed invitation.

**Texture & Light** — Grayscale photography that blooms into color on hover, cinematic letterboxing on the hero, soft vignette gradients, and a gold gradient text treatment used sparingly — only on the words that matter most.

**Motion** — Every section reveals itself gently as the visitor scrolls: timelines draw themselves, frames drift in slow independent orbits, progress lines trace across the screen. Nothing moves without purpose, and nothing moves too fast.

## VII. Responsive Experience

The cinematic feel is preserved across every screen size, not just desktop:

- The Memory Wall's floating constellation of photographs gracefully reorganizes into a clean two-column grid on mobile, preserving the same intimate captions
- The Love Stories timeline collapses from a two-column scroll narrative into a single vertical flow on smaller screens, with the gold connector line hidden in favor of stacked cards
- The Featured Films grid adapts from a four-column cinematic wall to two columns on tablets and a single column on phones, without losing the lightbox experience
- The navigation transforms into a full-screen, glass-blurred drawer on mobile, complete with its own closing tagline and quick access to booking
- The booking form's two-column layout reflows into a single column, keeping every field comfortable to fill on a touchscreen
